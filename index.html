Why Artemis exists

Cloud assistants are useful. Your desktop still deserves its own copilot.

Most AI tools feel disconnected from the real machine where your work happens. Artemis is being built as a local AI command center instead: voice, memory, dashboard, terminal, and app shell first, with a future downloadable desktop assistant as the long-term target.

The goal is not to make another chat tab. The goal is to make your local computer more legible, continuous, and eventually safer to control.

Privacy and safety

Local-first by default, action-safe by design.

Local models and local speech tooling are the default direction.

No tracking, analytics, remote scripts, remote images, or external fonts in this scaffold.

Private and no-memory modes remain product boundaries, not decorative labels.

Future computer control requires explicit approval, permissions, traces, and audits.

Roadmap

From local assistant to safe desktop command center.

  1. Now: local voice/STT/TTS, terminal UI, dashboard/status bridge, website, and app-shell preview.
  2. Next: real app shell scaffold that renders safe local status first.
  3. Later: Tauri desktop app, approval-gated computer-action layer, installer, and updater.
  4. Future: richer UI, safer automation, and optional integrations.
